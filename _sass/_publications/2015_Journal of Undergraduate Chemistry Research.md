# Short Proline-Substituted MUC1 Mucin Peptides Can Bind To Mouse MUC1 Monoclonal Antibody as Revealed by STD NMR
## Abstract
Many studies on the biological activity of mucin peptides derived from the VNTR domain that are considered as potential cancer vaccines have involved and focused on the immunodominant epitope PDTRP. A short peptide GVTSAPD preceding the main binding epitope and six other substituted peptides were investigated for their ability to bind mouse Muc1 monoclonal antibody (mAb, 6A4) by STD NMR. The Pro6 residue of GVTSAPD was replaced by Asp, Leu, Tyr, Phe, and Trp to obtain the Pro-substituted peptides. A sixth mutant peptide with Ser4 replaced by Asn was also studied for comparison. The results showed that substitution of Ser4 by Asn showed similar binding properties to the native peptide. The Pro-substituted peptides with position 6 occupied by Phe or Trp can bind the mAb, but not those containing Asp or Leu. The Pro-substituted peptides GVTSAFD and GVTSAWD may potentially be useful as additional epitopes toward the development of cancer vaccines.
