This recipe is heavily inspired by Serious Eats' [The Food Lab's Chocolate Chip Cookies](https://www.seriouseats.com/the-food-lab-best-chocolate-chip-cookie-recipe). Modified to suit my tastes.
#### Ingredients

2.25 sticks unsalted butter
1 ice cube

260 g flour
2 tsp salt
0.75 tsp baking soda

2 eggs
140 g granulated sugar
2 tsp vanilla extract
1 tsp caramel extract
0.5 tsp almond extract

140 g dark brown sugar

10 oz 72% cacao chocolate chips — Note: try to get the flat, wafer-shaped chips. This should result in a marbled appearance when baked. Ghirardelli makes these.


#### Directions

1)	Brown the butter in a medium saucepan on medium-low heat, stirring constantly (~6 min) — Note: the browning will occur quickly when it begins foaming.
2)	Whisk ice cube into butter. This will foam heavily so do this over the sink.
3)	Pour into separate bowl and let cool in fridge until edges are firm/opaque (~10 min).
4)	Whisk flour, salt, and baking soda together in large bowl
5)	Place granulated, eggs, and extracts into bowl of a stand mixer with whisk attachment. Whisk on medium-high until mixture has paled and falls off the whisk in ribbons (~5 min).
6)	Replace whisk attachment with paddle attachment and mix brown sugar and cooled brown butter into egg mixture on low speed (~15 sec).
7)	Slowly add flour mixture while mixing on low speed until just combined (~15 sec).
8)	Add chocolate chips and mix on low speed (~15 sec).
9)	Preheat oven to 325 F and prepare baking sheets with parchment paper
10)	Spoon dough onto pan slightly larger than a golf ball. Leave the balls rough/unrounded to maximize caramelized surface area.
11)	Bake for 12-13 minutes.
12)	Top with a small pinch of flaky sea salt. Kosher salt will suffice.
13)	Let sit on pan for at least 5 minutes before moving to cooling rack
